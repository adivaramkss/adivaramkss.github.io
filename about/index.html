STORY OF MAIKAVE KSHEEROLPADAKA SAHAKARANA SANGHAM

Add info Add info

Maikave ksheerolpadaka sahakarana sangham ltd no f 1778 was registered on 11/02/1960 and started operation on 6/4/1960. It was headquartered in Maikave. In 1926, the migration began in Malabar. The migration increased in 1940s. The migration of the migrants from the 1960s was almost exhausted. The Areekode-Thamarassery State Roadway was built at Tipu Sultan's day. The beginning of the migration to the cottage was started in 1942. St. Mary's Primary School was established in 1946. St. Anthony C. M. He became a manager of the school and led the social work. The dairy cooperative council started with the help of Father. At the time of registering in 1960, the first member of the Maikave ksheerolpadaka sahakarana sangham and the president was Honorous Athanasius' father .The action started as 25 farmers and 100 liters of milk procurement. 20 cents of land was purchased near Vetenary hospital Maikave. Milk collected in the morning and evening. The milk was stored in Kozhikode Milk Union. The farmers were forced to take dairy farming as a major resource along with cash crops and street turpil. The milk was transported to Thamarassery. The team has been working with a group of APCOS since 1988. A number of projects are being implemented at the Sangham level to promote the development of dairy farmers and to keep the farmers in the field. Each of the stages of the Dairy community has been founded by Fr Anthony, TM Antony Kannambilil, PM Varghese Pallikkaparambil, KV Varghese Kannandai, EJ Mathai Eliyaichu Uluzhayil, NS Thomas Naduvil Puthuppu, VS Jacob Master Puthanalloor, PS John Master, KV Devasy Kunnummal, PC Peter Parikkippillil, and former Governing Council members, Retired as Secretary, Mr. TJ John, the staff and the supervisors and the pensioners have played a key role in the success of this group. At present, Mr. C. J. Paulose serves as the Secretary, President Sri V K Jose Valiyapparambil and the members of the Governing Body works as the team. They have been able to greatly advance with the sincere efforts of the staff and supervisors. This group was developed by the migrant farmers to promote their livelihood. At present, a new generation of poultry farmers come up with a mere 5 Crore turnover and immovable products worth Rs 1 crore. There is no doubt that these will make Maikave ksheerolpadaka sahakarana sangham to contribute highly for the overall development of the state.
